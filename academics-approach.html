Academics

How We Teach.
Why It Works.

Northside's instructional model is built on one conviction: every student can reach college readiness when they are taught with consistency, high expectations, and genuine care.

Our Philosophy

Learning That
Goes Beyond the Test.

At Northside, we believe that strong academic outcomes come from strong relationships. Our teachers know their students by name, by strength, and by need. That knowledge drives every instructional decision we make.

We combine a rigorous, standards-aligned curriculum with culturally responsive pedagogy, meaning our students see themselves in their coursework and connect learning to their lives and communities. Instruction is differentiated so every student is challenged and supported at the right level.

Data informs our teaching but does not define our students. We use assessment results to adjust instruction quickly, identify students who need additional support, and ensure no one falls behind without intervention. The goal is mastery, not compliance.

Instructional Model

Six Pillars of
Northside Instruction.

These principles guide every classroom at Northside, from ninth grade through senior year.

01

High Expectations for All

Every student at Northside is held to the same high academic standard. We do not lower expectations — we raise support to meet them.

02

Data-Driven Instruction

Teachers analyze student performance data in weekly collaborative teams to identify trends, adjust pacing, and design targeted interventions before students fall behind.

03

Culturally Responsive Teaching

Our curriculum connects to the backgrounds, identities, and experiences of Brooklyn students. Students learn better when they see themselves in the material.

04

Differentiated Support

From enrichment for advanced learners to small-group intervention for students who need more time, instruction is matched to where each student is, not where we wish they were.

05

Collaborative Teacher Teams

Northside teachers plan together, observe one another, and share what works. Professional learning communities meet weekly across departments to refine instructional practice.

06

College-Ready Standards

All coursework is aligned to New York State standards with an eye toward college readiness. Regents preparation is embedded throughout — not bolted on at the end.

Inside the Classroom

What Learning Looks
Like at Northside.

Small Class Sizes

Intentionally small classes allow teachers to call on every student, provide written feedback on every assignment, and build the kind of trust that makes rigorous learning possible. No student is anonymous here.

Structured Academic Discourse

Students are taught to read closely, write argumentatively, and discuss ideas with precision. These skills cross every subject area and are essential for college-level work.

Intervention and Enrichment

Students who are struggling receive targeted small-group intervention during the school day. Students who are excelling are pushed into deeper work, not just more work.

Technology Integration

Students at Northside have access to current technology as a tool for learning, research, and creation. Digital literacy is embedded across the curriculum as a 21st-century skill.
