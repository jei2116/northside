Enrollment Documents

What You Will Need.

These documents are required to complete enrollment after acceptance. Our team will guide every family through the process. Nothing is collected at the time of application.

Required

Academic Transcripts

Most recent report card or official transcripts from the student's current school. We accept unofficial copies during enrollment.

Required

Proof of Age

Birth certificate, passport, or any government-issued document confirming the student's date of birth.

Required

Proof of Residency

Two documents showing current New York City address, such as a utility bill, lease agreement, or bank statement.

Required

Immunization Records

Up-to-date immunization records as required by New York State law. The school nurse can assist families who need help obtaining these.

If Applicable

IEP or 504 Plan

If your student has an active Individualized Education Program or 504 Plan, please provide a copy so we can prepare appropriate support from day one.

If Applicable

English Learner Records

If your student is an English Language Learner, any existing language assessments or translation records will help us place them appropriately.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions.

Is Northside really free?

Yes. Northside Charter High School is a public charter school fully funded by the state of New York. There is no tuition, no application fee, and no required donations. Families pay nothing to attend.

Does my child need to pass a test to get in?

No. There are no entrance exams, academic requirements, or interviews. Admission is determined by the New York City school lottery, which treats all applicants equally.

Does my child have to live in Brooklyn?

Any student residing in New York City is eligible to apply. Brooklyn residents are given enrollment priority, but students from other boroughs may apply and are admitted on a space-available basis after the lottery.

What grades does Northside serve?

Northside Charter High School serves students in grades 9 through 12. Students may apply to enter at any grade level, subject to seat availability.

What is the NYC school lottery and how does it work?

The NYC school lottery is a randomized process used when the number of applicants exceeds available seats. Every applicant who submits by the deadline is entered. Winners are selected at random and notified by mail. Being waitlisted does not mean a student will not be admitted - spots often open up as families make final decisions.

My child has a learning disability or IEP. Can they still apply?

Absolutely. Northside is fully inclusive and provides services for students with Individualized Education Programs and 504 Plans. We have special education teachers, counselors, and liaisons on staff. We encourage all families to share relevant records early so we can prepare the right supports from day one.

My child is an English Language Learner. Is Northside a good fit?

Yes. Northside has dedicated ESL staff and instructional support for English Language Learners. We serve a diverse student population and are experienced in supporting multilingual families through the application and transition process.

How do I schedule a private school tour?

Call us at (347) 390-1273 or email familyfeedback@northsidechs.org to schedule a private visit. You can also attend one of our Saturday Open Houses in May, which require no prior appointment.

When does the 2026-2027 school year begin?

The 2026-2027 school year begins on September 1, 2026. Enrollment confirmation, orientation schedules, and back-to-school information will be sent to accepted families in advance.
