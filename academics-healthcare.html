Pathway.

A dedicated career pathway that prepares Northside students for the healthcare industry through rigorous coursework, industry certifications, and real clinical experiences.

Program Overview

A Head Start
in Health Careers.

The Northside Healthcare Pathway is a multi-year program for students who want to explore careers in medicine, nursing, public health, and allied health fields. Students in the pathway take specialized coursework that goes beyond the standard curriculum and graduate with both a high school diploma and industry-recognized credentials.

The pathway is open to all Northside students beginning in 10th grade. No prior health experience is required — just a genuine interest in the field and a commitment to the additional coursework.

Curriculum

Three Years.
One Direction.

The pathway builds year over year, moving students from foundational knowledge to clinical exposure to professional readiness.

Grade 10 — Foundation

Introduction to Health Science

  • Medical terminology and anatomy
  • Body systems and disease
  • Introduction to healthcare careers
  • Health ethics and patient rights
  • Basic first aid and safety
Grade 11 — Applied Skills

Clinical and Technical Skills

  • CPR and first aid certification
  • Infection control and safety protocols
  • Patient care fundamentals
  • Healthcare technology and records
  • Introduction to clinical settings
Grade 12 — Professional Readiness

Internship and Capstone

  • Clinical internship at a partner site
  • Healthcare capstone project
  • College application support for health programs
  • Professional portfolio development
  • Industry mentor program
Credentials

Leave With More
Than a Diploma.

Pathway students earn industry-recognized credentials alongside their high school diploma, giving them a real advantage in college and the job market.

CPR and First Aid Certification

Students earn CPR and First Aid certification through an accredited provider, a credential required by virtually every healthcare employer and many college health programs.

Health Science Industry Credential

Graduates of the full three-year pathway receive a Health Science industry credential recognized by NYC health and workforce development programs.

Clinical Hours Documentation

Verified clinical internship hours are documented for students' college applications and future healthcare training programs, where hands-on experience is often required for admission.

Professional Portfolio

Every pathway graduate produces a professional portfolio documenting their skills, experiences, and goals — a valuable asset for college interviews and scholarship applications.
