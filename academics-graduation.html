Academics

Graduation
Requirements.

Every Northside student has a clear path to graduation. Here is exactly what it takes to earn your diploma, pass your Regents exams, and walk across that stage.

Diplomas

Your Diploma
Options.

Northside students graduate with a New York State Regents Diploma. Students who meet higher exam score thresholds and additional course requirements earn an Advanced Regents Diploma, which strengthens college applications and may qualify students for advanced placement in college coursework.

All Northside graduates also leave with a confirmed college acceptance. That is not a hope — it is a school-wide commitment backed by our college counseling program and four-year support structure.

Standard Track

NYS Regents Diploma

65 or higher on required Regents exams. Minimum 22 credits across core subject areas. Required for all Northside graduates.

Advanced Track

Advanced Regents Diploma

65 or higher on all required Regents exams plus additional math and science exams. Recommended for students pursuing competitive colleges or STEM programs.

Northside Guarantee

College Acceptance

Every Northside graduate has a confirmed college acceptance before graduation day. This is a condition of completion, not just a goal.

Credit Requirements

22 Credits.
Four Years.

New York State requires a minimum of 22 credits for graduation. Northside structures these credits across four years to keep every student on track.

Subject Area Credits Required Courses
English Language Arts 4 credits ELA 9, ELA 10, ELA 11, ELA 12
Social Studies 4 credits Global History I & II, US History, Government
Mathematics 3 credits Algebra I, Geometry, Algebra II / Pre-Calculus
Science 3 credits Living Environment, Earth Science or Chemistry, elective
Languages Other Than English 1 credit Spanish I or equivalent
The Arts 1 credit Visual Art, Music, or equivalent
Health 0.5 credits Health Education
Physical Education 2 credits PE (every semester, 9-12)
Electives 3.5 credits Includes Healthcare Pathway, Career Readiness, and others
Total 22 credits Minimum required for NYS Regents Diploma
Regents Exams

State Exams
You Need to Pass.

New York State requires passing scores on five Regents exams for a standard Regents Diploma. Northside prepares students for these exams throughout the year, not just in the weeks before.

English Language Arts (ELA)

Taken at the end of Grade 11. Tests reading, writing, and analytical skills across literary and informational texts.

Passing: 65+

Algebra I (or higher)

Most students take this at the end of Grade 9. Students pursuing the Advanced Diploma also take Algebra II and Geometry Regents.

Passing: 65+

Global History and Geography II

Taken at the end of Grade 10. Covers world history from 1750 to the present with a focus on document-based analysis.

Passing: 65+

United States History and Government

Taken at the end of Grade 11. Covers American history from the colonial period to the present, including document-based questions.

Passing: 65+

Science (Living Environment or Physical)

Living Environment is taken by most students in Grade 9 or 10. Students on the Advanced track also complete a physical science Regents.

Passing: 65+

Retakes and Support

Students who do not pass on the first attempt receive targeted intervention and may retake any Regents exam in January, June, or August.

Full support provided
Northside Requirements

Beyond the State.
The Northside Standard.

In addition to state requirements, Northside has three school-wide graduation requirements that distinguish our graduates.

01

Confirmed College Acceptance

Every student must have a confirmed acceptance to a two-year or four-year college or university before graduation. Our college counselor works with each student individually to make this happen.

02

Senior Internship

All seniors complete a supervised internship with a partner organization in Brooklyn or New York City. This experience is documented and may fulfill the Career Readiness elective credit.

03

Senior Portfolio and Presentation

Seniors present a portfolio of their four-year academic journey to a panel of teachers and community members. This culminating experience reinforces communication skills and college readiness.
